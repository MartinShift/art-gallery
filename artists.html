Jack B. Yeats

Jack B. Yeats (1871–1957) was an Irish painter and the brother of the poet W.B. Yeats. Known for his vibrant, emotive style, Yeats' work captures the heart of Irish life, landscapes, and folklore. His later works, often featuring bold colors and thick, expressive brushstrokes, reflect a unique vision of Ireland, blending realism with symbolism. His most famous pieces, such as "The Liffey Swim" and "The Funeral of Harry Boland", focus on both the beauty and struggles of Irish society. Yeats' art played a significant role in shaping modern Irish painting, and his works continue to be highly regarded for their depth and emotional resonance.

John Singer Sargent


​John Singer Sargent (1856–1925) was an American artist known for his remarkable skill in portraiture. Renowned for his ability to capture the personality and character of his subjects, Sargent's works are celebrated for their exquisite technique and mastery of light and texture. His most famous paintings include portraits of aristocrats and socialites, as well as dynamic works like "Carnation, Lily, Lily, Rose" and "Portrait of Madame X". Sargent’s ability to infuse his portraits with emotional depth and elegance has made him one of the most respected portrait painters of the 19th and early 20th centuries.

​Sir John Lavery 

​was an Irish-born Scottish artist known for his portraiture and historical scenes. Renowned for capturing the personality and emotions of his subjects, he painted notable figures such as Queen Victoria and Winston Churchill. His famous works include "The Artist's Studio: Lady Hazel Lavery with her Daughter Alice" and "Michael Collins, Love of Ireland", reflecting his connection to Irish history. Lavery's skill in light and detail made him one of the leading portrait painters of his time.
